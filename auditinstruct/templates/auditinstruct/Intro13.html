Example 3:

Suppose the true count is 59 croissants and 41 muffins, the bill of lading matches, and you choose to sample 15 items, as in example 1. However, in this case, the sample contains 7 croissants, which means the sample's estimated mix of the box is 47 croissants and 53 muffins. In this case, because the count of 59 croissants is outside the range of 37-57 (the sample's estimate of 47 croissants plus/minus 10 croissants), the shipment is automatically rejected. Because your sampling results lead you to reject a correct shipment (a wrong disagreement), your earnings computation will be:

50 (revenue) – 15 (sampling costs) – 5 (penalty for wrong disagreement) = 30 dollars.
