Example 2:

Suppose the true quantity of goods is 59 croissants and 41 muffins, as in example 1, but the bill of lading reports an inflated count of 79 croissants. Suppose also that you decide to sample 15 items of which 8 are croissants, so the sample's estimate is 53 croissants and 47 muffins in the shipment. In this case, because the count of 79 croissants is outside the previously described range of 43-63 croissants, the shipment is automatically rejected.
