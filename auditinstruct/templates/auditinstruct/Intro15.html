It is important that you understand the decision rule for rejecting a box of baked goods. The results of your sampling will cause you to reject automatically for two reasons:

(1) If the bill of lading reports a quantity of croissants that is NOT within +/- 10 croissants of the sample’s estimated count, you will automatically reject the daily shipment.

(2) If the bill of lading’s reported quantity equals 100 or 0 croissants, you will also automatically reject the box. In fact, because the odds of a miscount are so high when a bill of lading says only one type of item is present in the box, you will not be required to make a sampling decision when a reported count of 100 or 0 croissants is given. Instead, you will automatically reject the box and incur no sampling costs.

It is also important that you understand the decision rule for accepting a box of baked goods. Your sampling will cause the box to be automatically accepted for one reason:

(1) If the bill of lading reports a quantity of croissants that is less than or equal to the sample’s estimated count of croissants +/- 10 croissants, you will automatically accept the daily shipment.

In this study, the greater number of items you sample, the higher sampling cost you will pay. However, the greater number of items you sample, the more accurate will be your sampling estimate of the true distribution of the box, thus reducing the number of incorrect decisions.
