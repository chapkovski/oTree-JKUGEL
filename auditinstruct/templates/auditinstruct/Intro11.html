The following examples illustrate the net earnings computation. Because the examples merely illustrate the earnings computation, you should not view the hypothetical actions as indicative of the best thing for you to do.

Example 1: Suppose the 100 items consist of 59 croissants and 41 muffins. Suppose also that the bill of lading reports the true proportion. You decide to sample 15 items. The sample contains 8 croissants, or 53% of the items sampled. We extrapolate that ratio to an estimated shipment of 53 croissants and 47 muffins. Thus, the results of the sample are within +/- 10 items of the bill of lading’s claim.

To determine whether to accept or reject the shipment, the sampling procedure automatically compares the reported quantity to the sample's estimated quantity. If the reported quantity is within the range of the sample's estimate plus or minus 10 items, the shipment will be accepted. Thus, in this example, because the reported count of 59 croissants is within the range of 43-63 croissants (which is the sample's estimate of 53 croissants plus/minus 10 croissants), the shipment is automatically accepted. Because your sampling results lead you to accept a correct shipment, your earnings computation for this round will be: 50 (revenue) – 15 (sampling cost) = 35 dollars.
